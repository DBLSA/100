100 from 100

KICKSTARTING PART 0 AND PART 4


100 from 100 calls for support from the largest 100 UK architectural practices asking them to pledge £100,000 between them by 31 March 2022, to fund the development of pilot programmes for two LSA initiatives and their ongoing delivery.


What is 100 from 100?


Launched on 1 March 2022, 100 from 100 is a fundraising campaign to kickstart the immediate development of two new major LSA initiatives:


  • ‘Part 0’

    For young aspiring architects (aged 13-19) – early interventions in design education


  • ‘Part 4’

    For trained practitioners – raising skills and competencies


    If the largest 100 UK practices can answer this call to raise a minimum of £100,000 collectively, the LSA will begin immediate work on developing the initial pilot programmes as set out in the following pages.

    Part 0 – Meeting Social Value and EDI Ambitions & Requirements

    The Situation

    For practice, Social Value and EDI requirements are increasingly common in procurement processes and as a result there are many good initiatives designed to open up the profession to underrepresented groups in architecture.

    The Complication

    For teachers and career advisors messaging can be inconsistent, resource appears fragmented across different platforms and sector delivery is unstructured.

    Other professions and disciplines related to design and the built environment are leaving architecture trailing behind in attracting talent, with clear message alignment and structure.

    The Challenge

    We must act immediately by arming secondary school teachers and careers advisers with consistent and up-to-date knowledge and resources about architecture and the built environment to pass on to the next generation of aspiring practitioners.

    Our Solution

    In response to this, the LSA Part 0 will provide toolkits, workshops, lesson plans, learning materials, structured learning and networking offers for young people.

    Practices can use these resources in their own outreach work under a singular recognised flagship outreach programme, branded as Part 0.

    The Impact

    Part 0 creates a collective, unified and aligned approach to early intervention and outreach. The LSA will become an educational ‘clearing house’ for opportunities and programmes that provides our sector with the structure needed for meaningful impact in attracting the talent we seek.

    For Practice, the LSA Part 0 will be a comprehensive framework that supports practice in meeting Social Value and EDI ambitions and requirements.


    Your Recognition

    Your practice will;


  • Be recognised as an LSA Part 0 Delivery Partner

  • Receive brand recognition on the programme outputs

  • Be able to use the commitment of this donation to support your CSR, ED&I and ESG requirements on bids and tenders

    What will 100 from 100 will help us to deliver for Part 0?




    External Project Qualification (EPQ) Support Service


    Aimed at A-Level students, we will work with partner schools to provide EPQ supervision, enabling students to be armed with a developed project when applying to university or higher education courses


    Partner Programmes


    Partnerships that will join with existing outreach activities to align messaging and collectively promote careers in the built environment


    Mini-Modules


    Providing digital learning courses for students to take in the rudiments of built environment disciplines


    Workshops


    Engaging activities for learners, delivered by practice and/or the LSA in a structured and consistent format


    Information and guidance for career advisors

    Training sessions for teachers and careers advisors.


    Aimed to provide clarity and consistency in the messaging from the architectural community


    Curriculum Inserts


    Lesson plans and learning materials for teachers

    • A National Saturday Club (13-16 yr olds)

    • Accelerate Programme (16-19 yr olds)

    Part 4 – Raising Skills & Competencies Through Lifelong Learning

    The Situation

    In an ever-competitive recruitment market, Practices want to provide competitive staff benefits and retain talent. From a productivity perspective it is better to upskill from within.


    The Complication

    However, through consultation with our Practice Network, we have recognised that good quality CPD and training is difficult to find and can be time consuming in seeking out.


    Our Challenge

    Our Practice Network has challenged us to develop our Collective Peer Development programme and support them in designing the training and development modules that are currently missing in the sector.


    Our Solution

    In response, the LSA plans to develop a comprehensive post-qualification Part 4 programme, that will arm practitioners with tools to maintain and demonstrate competency.


    Delivered in the form of modular short-courses and training programmes they aim to support practice in realising design excellence from within and deliver on their training and development needs.


    Covering CPD, the new RIBA Mandatory Core Competencies and specialisms and core business skills, the programme will respond to the ARB and RIBA’s renewed emphasis on ‘competence’ and through consultation with practice will be bespoke to the needs and learning methods of trained designers.


    The Impact

    We aim to save practice time and resource by providing a one-stop shop for their staff training and development needs whilst further developing the skills and competencies of practitioners.


    Your Recognition

    Your practice will;

  • be recognised as Partners in the development of the Part 4 courses.

  • (optional) receive free access to the initial courses to the value of their donation

  • (optional) receive a 10% discount from all future LSA Part 4 courses.

What will 100 from 100 will help us to deliver for Part 4?

Three pilot short-courses which become revenue generating and self-sustaining for future development of the Part 4 programme. The format will be modular and set at the pace of the learner and aim to be 8 hours a week over4-6 week accessed digitally.



Short-course 1:

Life and Fire Safety


5 years after the tragedy of the fire at Grenfell Tower, the Building Safety Bill edges towards the statute book. This course will serve as a ‘refresher’ covering;

  • Core principles of fire safety and design

  • CDM regulations

  • The introduction of the latest thinking and best practice around fire safety.


Short-courses 2-3:

Nothing New: Heritage and Retrofit


This 2 part series of courses will;

  • Reflect on the need to work with what we already have to reduce net carbon

  • Encourage thinking on the increasingly contested field of heritage and the historic environment

  • Explore business development opportunities for fledgling practices

  • Connect learners with social enterprise and community groups outside of London working with heritage assets

  • Introduce radical perspectives on heritage practice

  • Introduce techniques of participatory design in heritage projects, and funding opportunities to make change happen.
